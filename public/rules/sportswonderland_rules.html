SPORTSWONDERLAND — ALL-STAR PICKUP

Official Rules ● Ages 4–7

WHAT THIS GAME IS ABOUT

  • The robot must touch 5 sports items on the map.
  • Each item is worth points.
  • The robot then drives back to the Huddle Pad to finish.
  • The whole match is 120 seconds (2 minutes).

1. The Story

It is game day at Capelli Sports. The team needs its equipment — helmets, whistles, and balls — before the match starts. Your robot is the helper that picks them up.

The robot drives around the field, touches each item, and then returns home to show that the team is ready to play.

2. The Field

2.1 Huddle Pad (Start and Finish)

  • The Huddle Pad is the dashed white box at the bottom of the map.
  • The robot starts inside the Huddle Pad.
  • At the end of the match, the robot must come back inside the Huddle Pad to earn a bonus.

2.2 The 5 Items (Tokens)

Five items are placed on the map. Each one gives a different number of points if the robot touches it:

  • Helmet — 5 points
  • Whistle — 4 points
  • Basketball — 3 points
  • Volleyball — 2 points
  • Tennis Ball — 1 point

2.3 Cones (Obstacles)

  • There are 4 cones on the field.
  • Cones are obstacles. The robot should drive around them.
  • Touching a cone makes the team lose 1 point and the Judge resets the mTiny to its starting position while the timer is running.

3. Before the Match Starts

  1. The combination is chosen and is the same for all the students (Manual and Automatic) in the same round. This combination shows where the 5 items and 4 cones go on the field.
  2. The referee places everything on the field exactly as the combination shows.
  3. The student places the robot fully inside the Huddle Pad, facing forward.
  4. The referee checks that everything is ready.
  5. The referee says: “3... 2... 1... GO!”

4. How to Play

4.1 Two Ways to Control the Robot

  • Manual mode (ages 4–5): the students scan the code blocks up, down, left, and right to control the robot.The student does NOT touch the robot itself.
  • Autonomous mode (ages 6–7): the student builds a program with cards before the match. Once the cards are scanned, the robot runs the program by itself.

4.2 Programming Time (Autonomous Mode Only)

  • The student gets 120 seconds (2 minutes) to lay out the cards.
  • If the student finishes early, they can say "Finish!" and the timer stops.
  • After the time ends or the student says "Finish," the cards cannot be changed anymore.
  • The Judge will write down the time for the construction of the algorithm.
  • Then the student scans the cards and the robot runs. (the time of scanning is not counted within the 120 seconds)

4.3 Touching Items

  • To collect an item, the robot just needs to touch its base. Bumping it, knocking it over, or driving over it all count.
  • Once an item is touched, the referee picks it up and removes it from the field.
  • Only the 5 items count. Cones do not give points.

4.4 Returning to the Huddle Pad

  • After collecting items, the robot should drive back to the Huddle Pad.
  • To get the bonus, the robot must be FULLY inside the dashed Huddle Pad when the match ends.

5. What is Not Allowed

  • After GO: nobody (except the referee) may touch the robot.
  • After GO: nobody may touch the items on the field.
  • After GO: nobody may move or reset things on the field.
  • Important: if anyone except the referee touches the robot or an item during the match, the team loses the round.

6. When the Match Ends

  • The match ends after 120 seconds, OR when the team says "STOP."
  • The robot must stop moving immediately.
  • The referee then writes down the final score.

7. Scoring

7.1 Points

  • Each item touched: gives its point value (1 to 5 points).
  • Robot fully inside Huddle Pad at the end: +5 bonus points.

7.2 Maximum Score

If the robot collects all 5 items AND returns fully to the Huddle Pad:

  • All 5 items: 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 = 15 points
  • Huddle Pad bonus: +5 points
  • Total: 20 points

8. Rounds and Ranking

  • Every team plays 2 rounds.
  • The best score of the 2 rounds is used for ranking.
