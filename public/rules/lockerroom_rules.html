STARTER — LOCKER ROOM MISSION

Official Rules ● Ages 13–15
Duration 150 seconds

WHAT THIS GAME IS ABOUT

  • The robot is the team's logistics helper before a big match.
  • It carries the Team Box from the locker room to the Coach for approval.
  • Then it scans a card to learn the team color (RED or GREEN).
  • It picks up that color cube and delivers it to open the stadium gate.
  • Finally, it sorts 3 colored tokens (RED, GREEN, BLUE) inside the maze.
  • The robot works on its own — no remote control.

1. The Story

It is match day. The team is getting ready in the locker room. Your robot is the assistant in charge of the team's gear.

First, the robot brings the Team Box to the Coach. The Coach approves the team and announces the team color for the day. Then the robot delivers that color's kit to the right field location, which opens the stadium gate. Once the gate is open, the robot finishes by sorting the rest of the equipment inside the maze.

2. The Field

2.1 Locker Room (Start Zone)

  • Located at the bottom-left of the field.
  • Marked "Capelli Locker Room Robotic Mission."
  • At the start of the match: the robot must be FULLY inside this zone AND must be touching or carrying the Team Box.

2.2 Scan Station (Bottom-Right)

  • The robot drives here while still carrying the Team Box.
  • The referee places a color cube (RED or GREEN) in front of the robot.
  • The robot scans the card by itself — no human help.
  • After scanning, the referee removes the cube and lifts the Team Box off the robot.

2.3 Big Delivery Circles

  • RED big circle: on the right side of the field. RED cubes go here.
  • GREEN big circle: in the lower middle area. GREEN cubes go here.

2.4 Stadium Gate

  • A small yellow barrier near the entrance to the maze.
  • It starts CLOSED.
  • The Judge opens it after the robot makes the color delivery.
  • Important: the robot must NOT touch the gate. Touching it loses 10 points.

2.5 Distribution Zones (A, B, C)

  • Three zones on the left side of the maze: Zone A, Zone B, Zone C.
  • Each zone holds ONE colored token (RED, GREEN, or BLUE).
  • Their placement is decided by a Zone Assignment card before the match (see §3).

2.6 Maze Color Targets

  • Inside the maze, there are 3 colored target rectangles: RED, GREEN, and BLUE.
  • Each colored cube must be delivered to its matching color target.
  • A Colored cube only counts if it ends FULLY inside its target (+10 pts).
  • If Partially inside the awarded point will be 5 points instead of 10 (+5pts).

3. Before the Match Starts

Two random cards are drawn:

3.1 Team Color Card

  • Decides the team color for this match: RED or GREEN.
  • This tells the robot which pickup zone to visit and which big circle to deliver to.

3.2 Zone Assignment Card

Decides where each colored token goes in Zones A, B, and C. There are 6 possible options:

  • Option 1: RED in A ● GREEN in B ● BLUE in C
  • Option 2: RED in A ● BLUE in B ● GREEN in C
  • Option 3: GREEN in A ● RED in B ● BLUE in C
  • Option 4: GREEN in A ● BLUE in B ● RED in C
  • Option 5: BLUE in A ● RED in B ● GREEN in C
  • Option 6: BLUE in A ● GREEN in B ● RED in C

3.3 Setup Steps

  1. Draw the Team Color card.
  2. Draw the Zone Assignment card.
  3. Place the tokens in zones A, B, and C as the card shows.
  4. Place the robot fully inside the locker room, touching the Team Box.
  5. Referee checks that everything is ready.
  6. Referee says: "3… 2… 1… GO!"

4. Team Box Attachment

Each team must build a part on their robot that holds the Team Box. This can be a hook, loop, frame, or anything else that:

  • Holds the Team Box steady while the robot drives.
  • Is fixed onto the robot before the match starts (cannot be added during the match).
  • Allows the referee to lift the Team Box off the robot at the Scan Station.

5. How to Play

The robot must complete the mission step by step. The referee tracks each step and gives points for each one done correctly.

Step 1 — Leave the Locker Room

  • The robot drives out of the locker room, carrying or in contact with the Team Box.
  • Reward: +5 points.

Step 2 — Reach the Scan Station

  • The robot drives to the Scan Station while still holding the Team Box.
  • Reward: +10 points.

Step 3 — Coach Approval

  • At the Scan Station, the robot scans the color card.
  • The referee removes the card.
  • The referee lifts the Team Box off the robot — this means the Coach has approved the team.
  • Reward: +15 points.

Step 4 — The 4-Second Pause

  • Cancelled. It’s preferred that the robot waits 4 seconds in order to let the referee remove the Team Box.

Step 5 — Deliver the Team Color Cube

Based on the color from the scan:

  • If RED was scanned: go to the RED pickup zone, get the RED cube, and deliver it to the big RED circle on the right side.
  • If GREEN was scanned: go to the GREEN pickup zone, get the GREEN cube, and deliver it to the big GREEN circle in the lower middle.
  • Correct delivery: +20 points.
  • Wrong color circle: −10 points.
  • Not delivered: 0 points.

Step 6 — Stadium Gate Bonus

  • If Step 5 was a CORRECT delivery, the gate is now open.
  • Bonus: +5 points.
  • If Step 5 was wrong or skipped, the gate stays closed and no bonus is given.

Step 7 — Sort the 3 Maze Tokens

The robot drives into the maze and visits zones A, B, and C. It picks up each colored token and delivers it to the matching color target inside the maze.

  • Each correct delivery: +10 points.
  • Each wrong delivery: −10 points.
  • Not delivered: 0 points.
  • Important: a token only counts if it ends FULLY inside its color target.
      • Half points will be counted if PARTIALLY inside the color target.

6. Robot Requirements

6.1 Allowed Robots

  • Educational robots (mBot2, CyberPi, etc.).
  • Battery only.
  • No sharp parts, no flames, no liquids, no projectiles.

6.2 Start Conditions

  • The robot must start fully inside the Locker Room, touching the Team Box.
  • The robot must NOT be holding any other cube at the start.

7. Autonomy Rule

After GO, the robot must run by itself. The student is NOT allowed to:

  • Touch the robot.
  • Use a remote control or joystick.
  • Move any cube or token by hand.
  • Adjust anything on the field.

The ONLY human action allowed during the match is the referee lifting the Team Box at the Scan Station.

8. Penalties and When the Run is Voided

8.1 Point Penalty

  • Robot touches the gate: −10 points.

8.2 Time Penalty

  • Cancelled

8.3 Run Voided (Score = 0)

The match score becomes 0 if any of these happen:

  • Anyone except the referee touches the robot or the field after GO.
  • The student uses a remote or joystick.

9. When the Match Ends

  • The match ends when the referee says "STOP."
  • The robot must stop moving immediately.
  • The referee checks each step and writes down the score.

10. Rounds and Ranking

  • Every team gets 2 runs.
  • The best score of the 2 runs is used for ranking.
